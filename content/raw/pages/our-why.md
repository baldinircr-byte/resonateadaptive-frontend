Hello, I am a music therapist working with individuals with spinal cord injuries at Mary Free Bed Rehabilitation Hospital in Grand Rapids, MI. As a music therapist, I use the way that music interacts with the neurologic, physical, and emotional systems in order to help individuals reach non-musical goals. This can include rehabilitation of walking, speech, cognition, etc. I work primarily with non-musicians, but also often find myself connecting with patients who are musicians and have lost their ability to play due to their injury. Recently I worked with a pianist considering how he could continue using the foot pedals while playing piano without function of his lower extremities. I would love to have access to a resonate 2.0 when it is available in order to better serve individuals such as this patient and offer them options for return to playing their instrument. Thank you! Bailey

Bailey

I’m so delighted to have come across this today. I’m a music student and have been playing piano since the age of 7 (I am 20 now). I also have a spinal injury and have used a wheelchair all my life. The fact I couldn’t use piano pedals was never really an issue until I started music school and I’ve been feeling a massive restriction since. I’ve tried all sorts of methods but nothing has worked for me and I was beginning to think that due to this, this was not a career path I could continue with but, discovering this has restored my faith and empowered my passion for the piano. I’d be so eager to try this product and think it would give me so much more opportunities. Thank you!
